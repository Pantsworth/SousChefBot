Ground Lamb Scotch Egg, Sweet Potato Fries and Tomato Tart - Recipe Finder

Ground Lamb Scotch Egg, Sweet Potato Fries and Tomato Tart

Info

  • Total Time:

    Under 4 Hours

  • Skill Level:

    Moderate

  • Cost:

    Moderate

Chef

Chef Art Smith View Profile
PRINT THIS RECIPE SHARE WITH A FRIEND

Top Chef Masters, Season 1, Episode 7, Elimination Challenge

Ingredients

GROUND LAMB SCOTCH EGG
  • 1/2 pound bacon, cut into pieces
  • 1/4 cup carrots, chopped
  • 1/4 cup onion, chopped
  • 1/4 cup celery, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 4 cups of ground lamb
  • Salt and pepper, as desired
  • 2 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil
  • 4 soft boiled eggs, peeled
  • 1/2 cup of Kentucky Bourbon Wiskey
  • 1 tablespoon of espresso powder
  • 1 cup of wine
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1/4 unsalted butter, softened
ROSEMARY AND BLEU CHEESE BISCUIT
  • 3 cups of self-rising flour
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup crumbled blue cheese
  • 1 teaspoon roasted crushed black pepper
  • 1/2 tablespoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons chopped rosemary
  • 1 cup of buttermilk
SWEET POTATO FRIES
  • 4 sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into French fry strips
  • 1 cup corn starch
  • Seasoning: 1 teaspoon chili powder, 1 teaspoon cumin powder, 1 teaspoon salt, 1 teaspoon garlic
TOMATO TART
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh ground ginger
  • 2 shallots, minced
  • 1 stick of celery, minced
  • 4 cups, fresh Heirloom tomatoes, either small ones or cut into 2 inch pieces
  • Salt and Pepper
TART DOUGH
  • 2 cups flour
  • Pinch of salt
  • Pinch of sage
  • 1/4 unsalted butter
  • Half cup of iced water

Directions

GROUND LAMB SCOTCH EGG

Sauté bacon in butter, add the vegetables and then cool. Mix in ground lamb and season with salt and pepper as desired. Divide lamb mixture into 4 burgers, place 1 peeled egg in each burger and seal. In a sauté pan with olive oil, heat to medium high, cook burgers to desired doneness, remove burgers to warm plate.

To make the red eye gravy, mix the bourbon and wine, reduce to half the amount. Add the espresso powder and brown sugar, reduce to half and whisk in butter. Serve with Rosemary and Bleu Cheese Biscuits.

ROSEMARY AND BLEU CHEESE BISCUIT

To make the dough, place flour in bowl, and whisk. Add butter and crumbled blue cheese and with fingers and work into dough. Add crushed black pepper, salt and rosemary, stir in buttermilk until it starts to make a ball.

Preheat oven to 400 degrees F.

Roll dough out and cut into 6 inch diameter and 2 inches thick, round disk. Place on parchment lined tray and back in oven for 25 minutes till brown and puffed. Split biscuits and place burgers on top and serve.

SWEET POTATO FRIES

Place sweet potato strips in bowl. Add corn starch and toss well. Remove strips; drop in preheated oil, about 350-375 degrees. Fry once, then before serving, fry second time to crisp, add seasoning and serve immediately.

TOMATO TART

Sauté ginger, shallots, and celery in butter for 2 minutes, add tomatoes toss add salt and pepper allow to cool and reserve. Place tomato mixture in a 9 inch pie dish.

TART DOUGH

In a bowl add the flour, salt, sage and whisk well.

Work the butter into the flour mixture and gradually add the iced water and stir with a fork. On a flour surface rolled dough out in a 1/4 of a inch and place on top of tomato mixture. Bake 400 degrees F until golden and bubbling, about 45 minutes.

Notes

Rosemary and Bleu Cheese Biscuits should be made ahead of time, before cooking burgers.
