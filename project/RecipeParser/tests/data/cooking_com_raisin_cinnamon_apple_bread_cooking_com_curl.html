Raisin-Cinnamon Apple Bread

  • Active Time 5m
  • Total Time 1h 15m

Makes 1 loaf (18 servings)

by Cooking at a Glance: Breads & Muffins

  • Pinterest
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Google+
  • Email
  • Print

Make this moist, spicy bread one day ahead, then serve it with tea, coffee, or cocoa the next afternoon. Substitute 1/2 cup dried cherries, currants or cranberries for the raisins for a deliciously different taste.

see coupons

ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup applesauce
  • 2 beaten eggs
  • 1/2 cup cooking oil
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 cup raisins or 1/2 cup chopped dried cherries or dried cranberries

directions

Grease a 9 x 5 x 3-inch loaf pan; set aside. In a large mixing bowl, stir together the flour, sugar, cinnamon, baking powder, baking soda and salt. In a medium mixing bowl, combine the applesauce, eggs, oil and milk. Add the egg mixture to the flour mixture; stir just till combined. Stir in the raisins, cherries or cranberries.

Pour the batter into the prepared pan. Bake in a preheated 350 degrees F oven for 60 to 65 minutes, or till a toothpick inserted near the center comes out clean. Cool in pan for 10 minutes. Remove from pan; cool thoroughly on a rack. Wrap and store overnight before slicing.
